In a system that reduces life to a chain of disconnected reactions to shock, personal com­ munication tends to lose all meaning... .The individual under terrorist conditions is never alone and always alone. I-Ie becomes numb and rigid not only in relation to his neighbor but also in relation to himself; fear robs him of the power of spontaneous emotional or mental reaction. Thinking becomes a stupid crime; it endangers his life. The inevitable conse­ quence is that stupidity spreads as a contagious disease among the terrorized population. Human beings live in a state of stupor, in a moral coma.
