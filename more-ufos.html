What happened? UFO Disclosure

#1 Falcon Lake Incident

(1967)
Stephen Michalak reported a close encounter
with a UFO near Falcon Lake in Manitoba, Canada.
He claimed to have been burned by the craft's
exhaust and provided detailed accounts of the incident.

2. Kecksburg UFO Incident

(1965)
Residents in Kecksburg, Pennsylvania, reported
seeing a fireball in the sky that supposedly crashed
in nearby woods. The incident is sometimes referred
to as "Pennsylvania's Roswell."

3. Ariel School UFO Sighting

(1994)
In Ruwa, Zimbabwe, over 60 school children reported
seeing a UFO and extraterrestrial beings near their
school. The incident gained international attention.

4. Phoenix Lights

(March 13, 1997)
Numerous people reported seeing a series of lights over
the skies of Phoenix, Arizona. The lights were described
as a triangular formation. The U.S. military (can you say
deutsche-bag?) initially claimed they were flares, but
intelligent witnesses remained unconvinced.

5. O'Hare International Airport UFO Sighting

(2006)
Multiple airport employees (over 10) and pilots (over 5)
reported a saucer-shaped object hovering over Gate C17 at
O'Hare International Airport in Chicago. The incident was
investigated by the FAA (jokingly).

Nimitz UFO Incident

(2004)
The U.S. Navy's USS Nimitz Carrier Strike Group
encountered unidentified aerial phenomena off the
coast of San Diego. Pilots reported seeing "Tic-Tac"
shaped objects exhibiting unusual flight characteristics.
